Question 8: What are the three main types of volcanoes, and give one example of each type?
Answer 8: The three main types of volcanoes are:

1. **Shield volcanoes:** These have broad, gentle slopes and are primarily formed by low-viscosity lava that can flow over long distances.
   Example: Mauna Loa in Hawaii.

2. **Stratovolcanoes (composite volcanoes):** These are steep-sided, symmetrical cones formed by alternating layers of lava flows, ash, and volcanic rocks.
   Example: Mount Fuji in Japan.

3. **Cinder cone volcanoes:** These are smaller, steep-sided cones built from volcanic fragments, mostly ash and cinders, that accumulate around a vent.
   Example: Paricutin in Mexico.
